The Fight to Save the Malayan Tiger

Eco-tourism revenues are currently funding crucial camera traps and anti-poaching patrols deep within the Belum-Temengor rainforest. The Malayan Tiger, an iconic symbol of our nation's wild heritage, is facing unprecedented threats from habitat loss and illegal poaching.

However, there is hope. Through dedicated conservation efforts led by both governmental and non-governmental organizations, significant strides are being made. Anti-poaching patrols, often employing indigenous communities who possess unrivaled knowledge of the forests, have increased dramatically.

How Eco-Tourism Helps

When you choose to visit protected areas like Belum-Temengor through responsible tour operators, your park fees directly contribute to these conservation efforts. The presence of eco-tourists also acts as a deterrent to poachers, who prefer empty, unmonitored forests.

The fight is far from over, but by shifting toward sustainable travel, we ensure that the roar of the Malayan Tiger continues to echo through our ancient rainforests for generations to come.
